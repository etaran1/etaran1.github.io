Internal Network Segmentation to Reduce Lateral Movement

Role Simulation: Security Engineer (Junior–Mid)
Engineering / Segmentation
Scenario

Flat internal network architecture increases risk of lateral movement following endpoint compromise.

Analysis & Decisions
  • Defined trust boundaries between user, server, and admin zones.
  • Designed VLAN segmentation and least-privilege firewall policies.
  • Explicitly restricted east-west traffic and privileged pathways.
  • Proposed monitoring points for high-value assets.
Design rationale: Segmentation reduces blast radius. Least-privilege rules limit lateral movement opportunities.
Outcome
  • Reduced attack surface and limited lateral movement paths.
  • Improved visibility and control over internal traffic flows.
Artifacts Produced
Network Diagram + Rationale Firewall Rule Justification Table Risk Reduction Summary
Reflection
  • IDS placement at boundaries increases detection capability.
  • Zero-trust segmentation would further reduce implicit trust.

Secure Remote Access Architecture Review

Role Simulation: Security Engineer (Junior–Mid)
Engineering / Access
Scenario

Remote access relies on VPN authentication without device posture validation or conditional controls.

Analysis & Decisions
  • Identified compromised endpoint risk and insufficient assurance controls.
  • Reviewed authentication strength and monitoring gaps.
  • Recommended layered improvements over full redesign (faster adoption).
  • Proposed restricted access scope + MFA + enhanced logging.
Recommendation: Layered controls (MFA + restricted scope + logging) reduce risk quickly without disrupting ops.
Outcome
  • Reduced exposure while maintaining business continuity.
  • Improved incident response readiness through deeper logging.
Artifacts Produced
Architecture Review Summary Control Recommendation List Logging & IR Readiness Notes
Reflection
  • Endpoint health checks improve assurance significantly.
  • Access telemetry should be centralized for correlation and triage.
