SOC Analyst (I–II) Simulations

Two role-based scenarios demonstrating alert triage, severity decisions, escalation rationale, and incident documentation artifacts.

Suspicious PowerShell Activity Investigation

Role Simulation: SOC Analyst I
SOC / Triage
Scenario

High-severity alert triggered by encoded PowerShell execution on a workstation used by a non-technical employee.

Analysis & Decisions
  • Correlated process execution evidence with user behavior and asset criticality.
  • Evaluated absence of persistence signals and lack of corroborating outbound traffic.
  • Compared activity against a benign baseline and known updater behaviors.
  • Downgraded severity and closed as a false positive with documented rationale.
Decision note: Alert downgraded due to no corroborating telemetry (no persistence + no suspicious outbound), and execution matched a known benign updater behavior pattern.
Outcome
  • Incident closed as false positive with supporting evidence.
  • Recommended rule tuning and enrichment to reduce future noise.
Artifacts Produced
Incident Investigation Report Triage Notes + Evidence Summary Severity Rationale Memo
Reflection
  • More endpoint telemetry would increase closure confidence.
  • Automated enrichment (hash/IP reputation) would shorten triage time.

Brute-Force Login Attempt with Lateral Movement Risk

Role Simulation: SOC Analyst II
SOC / Escalation
Scenario

Multiple failed authentication attempts followed by a successful login on a privileged account.

Analysis & Decisions
  • Validated anomalous timing and source reputation indicators.
  • Confirmed privileged access scope and potential blast radius.
  • Prioritized impact and privilege level despite limited indicators.
  • Escalated incident and recommended containment actions.
Escalation rationale: Privileged account + anomalous successful login following failures = high-impact risk, escalated for containment even with moderate confidence.
Outcome
  • Escalated for credential reset and access review.
  • Recommended MFA enforcement and conditional access controls.
Artifacts Produced
Escalation Report Auth Timeline + Findings Impact Assessment Summary
Reflection
  • MFA and stricter privilege boundaries would reduce likelihood.
  • Triage scoring should weight privilege level more heavily.
