Simulated Projects (Decision-Focused)

These are scenario-based projects designed to demonstrate analyst and engineering decision-making: what signals were observed, what was ruled out, what actions were recommended, and what artifacts were produced. The goal is to show judgment and documentation quality—not step-by-step tutorials.

What you’ll see: triage logic, risk reasoning, architecture tradeoffs
Outputs: incident notes, escalation memos, risk entries, diagrams
Style: evidence → decision → impact

Suspicious PowerShell Activity Investigation

Role Simulation: SOC Analyst I
SOC / Triage
Scenario

High-severity alert triggered by encoded PowerShell execution on a workstation used by a non-technical employee.

Analysis & Decisions
  • Correlated process execution evidence with user behavior and asset criticality.
  • Evaluated absence of persistence signals and lack of corroborating outbound traffic.
  • Compared activity against a benign baseline and known updater behaviors.
  • Downgraded severity and closed as a false positive with documented rationale.
Decision note: Alert downgraded due to no corroborating telemetry (no persistence + no suspicious outbound), and execution matched a known benign updater behavior pattern.
Outcome
  • Incident closed as false positive with supporting evidence.
  • Recommended rule tuning and enrichment to reduce future noise.
Artifacts Produced
Incident Investigation Report Triage Notes + Evidence Summary Severity Rationale Memo
Reflection
  • More endpoint telemetry would increase closure confidence.
  • Automated enrichment (hash/IP reputation) would shorten triage time.

Brute-Force Login Attempt with Lateral Movement Risk

Role Simulation: SOC Analyst II
SOC / Escalation
Scenario

Multiple failed authentication attempts followed by a successful login on a privileged account.

Analysis & Decisions
  • Validated anomalous timing and source reputation indicators.
  • Confirmed privileged access scope and potential blast radius.
  • Prioritized impact and privilege level despite limited indicators.
  • Escalated incident and recommended containment actions.
Escalation rationale: Privileged account + anomalous successful login following failures = high-impact risk, escalated for containment even with moderate confidence.
Outcome
  • Escalated for credential reset and access review.
  • Recommended MFA enforcement and conditional access controls.
Artifacts Produced
Escalation Report Auth Timeline + Findings Impact Assessment Summary
Reflection
  • MFA and stricter privilege boundaries would reduce likelihood.
  • Triage scoring should weight privilege level more heavily.

2 simulated risk cases

Insecure Data Storage Risk Assessment

Role Simulation: GRC / Risk Analyst
GRC / Risk
Scenario

Sensitive operational data stored on shared network drives without encryption or periodic access review.

Analysis & Decisions
  • Assessed confidentiality and compliance exposure based on data sensitivity.
  • Rated likelihood using access scope, role distribution, and audit gaps.
  • Prioritized mitigation over acceptance due to impact severity.
  • Proposed compensating controls: encryption + access review cadence.
Risk decision: Not acceptable as-is. Mitigation required due to broad access scope and high confidentiality impact.
Outcome
  • Risk documented, scored, and prioritized for remediation.
  • Recommended encryption-at-rest and quarterly access audits.
Artifacts Produced
Risk Register Entry Likelihood × Impact Scoring Mitigation Recommendation
Reflection
  • Formal data classification reduces scoring ambiguity.
  • Automated access reporting improves audit quality and cadence.

Third-Party Vendor Access Risk Evaluation

Role Simulation: GRC / Risk Analyst
GRC / Vendor
Scenario

Third-party vendor requests persistent network access for operational support.

Analysis & Decisions
  • Assessed vendor access scope, authentication strength, and monitoring gaps.
  • Identified over-privilege risk and weak time-bound controls.
  • Conditionally accepted risk with compensating controls and monitoring.
  • Recommended time-bound access, segmentation, and periodic review.
Residual risk approach: Acceptable only with compensating controls (time-bound access + monitoring + review cadence).
Outcome
  • Risk accepted with documented conditions and control requirements.
  • Proposed vendor access review cycle and least-privilege redesign.
Artifacts Produced
Risk Acceptance Memo Control Justification Notes Residual Risk Assessment
Reflection
  • Segmented vendor access reduces blast radius immediately.
  • Strong logging requirements improve incident response readiness.

Network Defense / Security Engineering

2 simulated design reviews

Internal Network Segmentation to Reduce Lateral Movement

Role Simulation: Junior Security Engineer
Engineering / Segmentation
Scenario

Flat internal network architecture increases risk of lateral movement following endpoint compromise.

Analysis & Decisions
  • Defined trust boundaries between user, server, and admin zones.
  • Designed VLAN segmentation and least-privilege firewall policies.
  • Explicitly restricted east-west traffic and privileged pathways.
  • Proposed monitoring points for high-value assets.
Design rationale: Segmentation reduces blast radius. Least-privilege rules limit lateral movement opportunities.
Outcome
  • Reduced attack surface and limited lateral movement paths.
  • Improved visibility and control over internal traffic flows.
Artifacts Produced
Network Diagram + Rationale Firewall Rule Justification Table Risk Reduction Summary
Reflection
  • IDS placement at boundaries increases detection capability.
  • Zero-trust segmentation would further reduce implicit trust.

Secure Remote Access Architecture Review

Role Simulation: Junior–Mid Security Engineer
Engineering / Access
Scenario

Remote access relies on VPN authentication without device posture validation or conditional controls.

Analysis & Decisions
  • Identified compromised endpoint risk and insufficient assurance controls.
  • Reviewed authentication strength and monitoring gaps.
  • Recommended layered improvements over full redesign (faster adoption).
  • Proposed restricted access scope + MFA + enhanced logging.
Recommendation: Layered controls (MFA + restricted scope + logging) reduce risk quickly without disrupting ops.
Outcome
  • Reduced exposure while maintaining business continuity.
  • Improved incident response readiness through deeper logging.
Artifacts Produced
Architecture Review Summary Control Recommendation List Logging & IR Readiness Notes
Reflection
  • Endpoint health checks improve assurance significantly.
  • Access telemetry should be centralized for correlation and triage.
